# EP-001 - Voiceover only (read-aloud script)

Welcome to ByteCast Day 1. Spoken lines only - no on-screen or stage directions. Canonical source: training plan Annex A, mirrored here for repo-local narration and listen-mode generation.

---

## Opening

Welcome to ByteCast.

This is not one giant app.

It is a connected learning system built from focused packs, thin routing shells, and a deliberate path.

If you are brand new here, that is good news, because this episode is built for the first day mind.

You do not need to know every division.

You do not need to understand every internal tool.

You do not need to click everything you can see.

Today your job is smaller and more useful:

understand what ByteCast is, how the current Golden Path actually works, and which main doors are safe to trust first.

---

## Scene 1 - What ByteCast is

ByteCast is the guided learning layer of this workspace.

It teaches.

It routes.

It checks understanding.

It records proof.

And it prepares the learner to move from exposure into repeatable work.

That is why the system is modular.

Instead of forcing every lesson, every tool, and every reference into one surface, ByteCast keeps the learner journey focused.

You enter through orientation.

You resume through the Playlist.

You branch into routes through Training Hub.

You build reusable artifacts through Seed Builder.

And you go to Docs when the answer needs to be governed and official.

That structure is not cosmetic.

It is the learning model.

---

## Scene 2 - The journey loop

Here is the loop.

Listen.

Slides.

Engage.

Training.

Seed.

Badge.

The important thing is not just memorizing the words.

The important thing is understanding what the loop is protecting.

Listen gives the learner orientation before detail.

Slides break the idea into visible pieces.

Engage checks whether the idea actually landed.

Training asks for stronger proof.

Seed turns learning into something reusable.

Badge makes completion visible.

And on the current Golden Path, that sequence is real.

EP-001 leads into EP-002, EP-003, EP-004, then TR-001A, then seed export, publish proof, and badge eligibility.

So when the system asks you to move in order, it is not being dramatic.

It is protecting meaning.

---

## Scene 3 - Your first mental model

Here is the mental model to keep:

ByteCast is a path, not a pile.

A pile says, here are a hundred things, good luck.

A path says, start here, prove this, then move forward.

That difference matters because beginners often mistake motion for progress.

Opening six tabs can feel productive.

But if you cannot answer what step you are on, what door you should use, and what the next recommended action is, you are probably drifting.

The safer move is usually to come back to the path and ask the smaller question:

what is the next honest step?

---

## Scene 4 - The five main doors

Now the five main doors.

Door one: Workspace Entry.

That is the orientation door.

It shows the shape of the workspace and helps new people understand what exists.

Door two: ByteCast Playlist.

That is the learner control room.

It is where episodes are resumed, gates are seen, and next recommended action is easiest to understand.

Door three: Training Hub.

That is the route map.

It helps answer questions like which mission, which track, which prerequisite, and which learning route fits the need.

Door four: Seed Builder Studio.

That is where repeatable artifact work belongs - templates, generators, exports, and builder-style workflows.

Door five: Docs Portal.

That is where governed answers live - standards, proof rules, troubleshooting, canon notes, and other references that need to be official.

Each door has a job.

The whole point of this episode is to keep those jobs from blurring together.

---

## Scene 5 - Learner-facing vs internal

Now an important boundary.

Not every visible surface is meant to be your Day 1 starting point.

Some pages are learner-facing.

Some pages are maintainer-facing.

Some files are raw config.

Some routes exist to support the system, not to onboard a new operator.

That is why ByteCast gives you public learner doors first.

It lets you build confidence without requiring you to interpret workflow internals, raw data, or system glue on the first pass.

So do not measure readiness by how deep you can wander.

Measure it by whether you can stay correctly routed.

That is a stronger skill.

## Scene 6 - The canonical starts

The start sequence is intentional.

EP-001 teaches the learning system itself.

EP-002 teaches the AeroVista ecosystem you are inside.

EP-003 teaches which door handles which job.

EP-004 teaches where truth lives and why trust labels matter.

That order is doing real work.

System first.

Company second.

Navigation third.

Truth fourth.

Only after that does the Day 1 mission ask you to prove understanding in a more durable way.

That is a cleaner learning progression than throwing the learner into every division and every tool on minute one.

---

## Scene 7 - What progress means here

Progress in ByteCast is proof-based.

Not click-based.

The early episode gates ask for listen, slides, and Engage.

The mission asks for a stronger check.

And later stages ask for artifact and publish proof.

That matters because the system is trying to distinguish three things:

completion,

understanding,

and proof.

Those are not the same.

If something is locked, the most useful assumption is not that the system is blocking you for no reason.

The useful assumption is that the sequence is trying to preserve context.

Success here is simple.

You know Playlist is the safest return surface after a break.

And you know Seed Builder is for repeatable output, not for random Day 1 wandering.

You do not need everything today.
