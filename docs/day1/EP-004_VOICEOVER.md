## Cold open

Welcome to EP-004 - Current Truth Basics.

EP-003 gave you the doors.

This episode gives you the truth layers.

Because opening the right door is not enough if you still treat every file, dashboard, and message thread as equally official.

Here is the problem in one line:

when nobody declares what is current, teams guess.

And guessing scales into rework, reversed decisions, and customer-facing mistakes.

We use three cooperating ideas:

ACOS for visibility.

Domain systems for ownership.

Source of Truth, or SOT, for declared trust.

By the end, you should be able to explain why newest file wins is not a governance strategy - and what you check instead.

Multiple lanes move at once.

Creative.

Technical.

Operational.

## Slide `s1` - why it matters

Ambiguity is expensive.

It does not always look dramatic.

Sometimes it looks like duplicate work.

Sometimes it looks like using the wrong draft.

Sometimes it looks like giving a learner or teammate the wrong answer because nobody labeled what was current.

That is why clarity matters early.

Your day-one job is not to memorize every system.

Your job is to learn where authority lives.

---

## Slide `s2` - ACOS

ACOS is best understood as a cockpit.

It helps you see the fleet.

It helps you see alerts, signals, and cross-lane status.

That visibility is valuable.

But a cockpit is not the same thing as every system of record behind it.

Visibility helps you orient fast.

Ownership still lives with the underlying domain systems.

So when a question becomes exact, binding, or high-stakes, visibility is not the final answer by itself.

---

## Slide `s3` - domain systems

Domain systems own the record.

Finance tools own the money truth.

Inventory tools own stock truth.

Support tools own ticket truth.

Operational trackers own delivery truth.

Dashboards can summarize those systems.

That is useful.

But replacing the owning system with the prettier summary is how subtle errors turn into commitments, invoices, and bad calls.

If the question touches money, legal exposure, or operational promises, confirm with the owning team and the owning system.

---

## Slide `s4` - SOT

SOT is where trust gets labeled.

Active.

Canonical.

Legacy.

Duplicate.

Archive.

Those labels matter because they stop people from inventing their own ranking system.

In the ByteCast workspace, labels like Primary, Legacy, Duplicate, and Archive are examples of this kind of trust language.

When two files disagree, governed source and declared status beat whichever file happened to be saved most recently.

Timestamps can help.

But timestamps are not governance.

---

## Slide `s5` - mental sort

Now the sort.

Memorize the jobs.

ACOS means visibility.

SOT means declared trust.

Work Orders mean execution.

Reporting means interpretation and signal surfaces.

Domain systems mean records of fact.

A training standard in docs belongs to the trust layer.

A dashboard status view belongs to the visibility layer.

A rule like new ByteCast episodes publish under episodes slash slug belongs to trust and governance, not to a dashboard.

That is the level of distinction the learner needs.

PAUSE.

## Slide `s6` - misuse

Three traps that look like productivity.

Trap one:

newest file equals official.

It does not.

Trap two:

opening legacy wrappers, random folders, or nearby copies instead of checking the canonical path.

Familiar does not mean current.

Trap three:

treating clicks as proof.

The Golden Path expects listen, slides, Engage, and recorded evidence for a reason.

The system is trying to protect meaning, not just movement.
