# EP-003 - Voiceover only (read-aloud script)

The Main Doors. Pair with the episode slide order. Spoken lines only - no stage directions except optional PAUSE cues for recording.

## Cold open

Welcome to EP-003 - The Main Doors.

In EP-001, you learned the learning system.

In EP-002, you learned the company map.

Now we make the workspace usable.

Not by memorizing URLs.

By memorizing jobs.

The question for this episode is simple:

which door matches what I need right now?

There are five learner-facing doors on the Day 1 map:

Workspace Entry.

ByteCast Playlist.

Training Hub.

Seed Builder Studio.

Docs Portal.

Everything else may still be useful later, but those are the doors the learner needs first.

PAUSE.

---

## Slide `s_open` - scope

This episode is intentionally narrow.

We are not debugging infrastructure.

We are not opening random internals to see what happens.

We are building a routing reflex:

task to door.

If you finish this episode and still justify opening five random apps to learn faster, we missed.

If you finish and start matching questions to the right surfaces before you click, we won.

---

## Slide `s1` - Workspace Entry

Door one: Workspace Entry.

Use it when you need orientation.

When you need the shape of the workspace.

When you need to remember how ByteCast, Training Hub, Builder, and Docs relate.

It is the lobby.

Not the workshop.

Strong first click for a new person.

Strong first click after a handoff when the map feels fuzzy.

Weak choice if you already know that your real question is about progress, standards, or artifact generation.

---

## Slide `s2` - ByteCast Playlist

Door two: ByteCast Playlist.

This is the learner control room.

Resume the current path.

Check gate status.

See the next recommended action.

Keep proof attached to the journey instead of trusting memory.

If someone says, pick ByteCast back up where you left off, Playlist is usually the first honest click.

It is not the whole training catalog.

It is the learner-facing spine of progress.

---

## Slide `s3` - Training Hub

Door three: Training Hub.

Think route map.

Think module map.

Think prerequisites, missions, and structure.

Use it when the question sounds like:

which training route exists?

what comes after this prerequisite?

which mission fits this role?

ByteCast gives you narrated spine and gated progression.

Training Hub gives you breadth.

That is why they work together.

---

## Slide `s4` - Seed Builder Studio

Door four: Seed Builder Studio.

This is the repeatable artifact door.

Templates.

Generators.

Export-oriented flows.

Builder work.

The day-one lesson is not to camp there.

The day-one lesson is to recognize the category:

if I am making something reusable and structured for handoff, Builder is probably the right home.

That is much safer than improvising the whole job in a random scratch surface.

---

## Slide `s5` - Docs Portal

Door five: Docs Portal.

When the answer must be official, start in docs.

Standards.

Proof rules.

Troubleshooting.

Workspace maps.

Canon notes.

Training standards and proof schema are good examples of the kind of answer that belongs there.

Docs is not just more text.

It is where the organization says, this is the governed reference.

---

## Slide `s6` - Learner vs internal

Now the guardrail.

Internal dashboards exist.

Maintainer tools exist.

Raw registries exist.

That does not make them the right Day 1 starting point.

Depth is not the same as readiness.

If you skip the routing model and jump straight to internals, you often get motion without understanding.

That is why the Golden Path uses public learner surfaces first.

Clarity before depth.

---

## Slide `s7` - Before you continue

Quick self-check.

Can you name all five doors?

Can you say one sentence about when each wins?

Resume progress - Playlist.

Need a governed standard - Docs.

Need route discovery - Hub.

Need orientation - Entry.

Need repeatable artifact work - Builder.

If that feels clean, good.

That is the routing reflex we wanted.

Next episode: EP-004 - Current Truth Basics.

Doors tell you where to go.

Truth layers tell you what to trust once you get there.
