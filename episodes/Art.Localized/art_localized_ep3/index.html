Ep 3 — Amplification

The marketing campaign pack is the public amplification layer: launch video, ambassador network, microsite, creator education, and the impact loop that keeps the movement legible outside the internal team.

Amplification starts with a human signal. The launch video frames the campaign around a simple claim: AI did not take the art, it gave it back. The public story has to feel human before it feels impressive.

The regional ambassador program turns that story into local presence. Artists, educators, and community leaders host events, share stories, and represent AeroVista in places like the Pacific Northwest, Metro Manila, Ontario, and Barcelona. Amplification works when real people carry it.

The microsite gives the movement a stable public surface: hero message, mission, artist stories, pilot preview, and join-the-movement funnel. That keeps the campaign from fragmenting into isolated posts.

Education and harvest close the loop. Summit Learning turns the message into accessible AI-for-local-creators curriculum, while the harvest protocol measures creator participation, feedback, partnerships, and long-term hubs. Good amplification widens the story and improves the system underneath it.

Slides

Human signal

The launch video defines amplification as a narrative move, not a volume move.

Line. “AI didn’t take the art. It gave it back.”
Scenes. Silent studios, awakening, empowerment, and call to action.
Rule. The public message must stay rooted in creators, not platform bragging.

Ambassadors are the movement’s local relay points. They make the campaign visible in communities, not just on screens.

Who. Artists, educators, and community leaders.
What. Events, stories, local representation, and workshops.
Why. Public trust rises when the campaign has real regional faces.

The microsite organizes the message into a repeatable public funnel instead of dispersing it across ad hoc links.

Hero. Mission and CTA anchor the message.
Stories. Testimonials, feature reels, and pilot preview deepen credibility.
Join. Signup and newsletter capture convert attention into participation.

Amplification is strongest when it also teaches and measures. Otherwise reach becomes noise.

Curriculum. Creative foundations, tools, ethics, and responsible use.
Harvest. Creator participation, feedback, and partnership growth.
Outcome. Signal should widen reach and improve creator equity at the same time.

Field guide

Use this before publishing a campaign beat. Amplification is not volume; it is the disciplined connection between a person, a story, a channel, and a next action.

Use the three-signal test. Every post or page section should include a human signal, a proof signal, and an action signal. If one is missing, the launch beat is probably weak.
Choose one primary metric. Do not hide behind vanity metrics. Pick the metric that matches the goal: signups, partner replies, workshop attendance, program-page clicks, or artist follow-up calls.
Protect context while simplifying. Short-form content can be concise without becoming generic. Keep the artist's region, medium, and intent visible.
