Ep 2 — Partnership tiers

Phase 3 scales the program through partner structure. Tiers are not cosmetic labels. They change the collaboration offer, the proof package, and the cross-division obligations that come with the deal.

Phase 3 scales Art Localized from a community initiative into a partnership-ready movement. The partner deck is clear about the offer: sponsorship of local showcases, co-branded events, and artist-in-residence programs become the vehicles for expansion.

Partnership tiers define how much commitment and infrastructure is involved. Community Partners include schools and local studios. Cultural Partners include regional institutions. Corporate Allies include media and tech collaborators. The tier determines how much coordination, proof, and shared output is required.

Division collaboration must stay explicit. Lumina handles campaign orchestration, EchoVerse owns sound identity, SkyForge develops immersive art experiences, Vespera handles publication, Summit drives educational impact, and Horizon captures regional showcases. Tier promises are only credible when those roles stay mapped.

Proof for partnership conversations lives in the press kit and metrics. The pack calls for logos, a fact sheet, media contacts, creator profiles, quotes, soundtrack links, and milestone data. A real tier should change both the artifact set and the reviewer path.

Tier shapes the offer

The partner deck turns “support” into concrete lanes, and each lane carries a different collaboration burden.

Community. Local schools, art studios, and grassroots activators.
Cultural. Institutions that expand legitimacy and regional reach.
Corporate Ally. Media or tech collaborators with larger amplification power.

Tiers are useful only if they drive different asks, different benefits, and different next steps.

Sponsorship. Fund and stage local showcases.
Co-branded events. Share audience and proof in public.
Residencies. Create higher-touch programs with deeper support.

Every partner promise crosses divisions, so the operating model has to name who owns what.

Lumina. Press kits, ads, social, and orchestration.
EchoVerse / SkyForge. Soundtrack plus immersive activations.
Vespera / Summit / Horizon. Publication, certification, and cinematic capture.

Higher-touch tiers need stronger proof. That means better artifacts, clearer metrics, and tighter reviewer paths.

Press kit. Logos, fact sheet, contacts, creator examples, and QR links.
Feedback data. Reach, partnerships, creator count, and follow-up activity.
Rule. If the tier rises, the artifact standard rises with it.

Field guide

Use this when deciding whether a partner helps the creator or simply borrows the campaign's credibility. A good partner tier increases support and proof without quietly shifting risk onto the artist.

Community Partner. Best for local workshops, venue access, small showcases, and trust-building. Proof can be simple: event plan, consented artist profile, and attendance or signup target.
Cultural Partner. Best for institutions, nonprofits, galleries, schools, or regional organizations. Proof needs stronger review: press kit, role map, public language, and a clear creator benefit.
Corporate Ally. Best only when larger reach adds real support. Require stricter proof: rights language, benefit terms, brand usage approval, measurable feedback, and an exit path if the fit turns extractive.
