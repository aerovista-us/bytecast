Ep 4 — Your role

Phase 4 turns interest into careful next steps. Your role is to route creators and collaborators into the right pathway, keep proof clean, and preserve creator equity while the system learns.

Phase 4 is where participation becomes a responsible next step. The pathway plan names the goal directly: creators should understand what is being proposed, what is still experimental, and what review is required before anything becomes public.

The rights framework defines the public surface. Creator profiles, intake records, usage notes, reviewer comments, and embeddable galleries turn the campaign into a careful pilot instead of a symbolic one.

Ecosystem integration makes every division accountable for a clear support role. Lumina can shape branded collaboration notes, EchoVerse can explore audio fit, Nexus can power dashboards and consent records, Summit can support education, Vespera can handle publication review, and Horizon can support visual capture when rights are clear.

Your role is to keep those mechanisms legible. Route the creator to the right lane, match the artifact to the reviewer path, and keep the metrics honest. If the work scales but creator equity disappears, the system failed.

Field guide

Use this when turning attention into a profile, workshop, partner conversation, publication draft, or future pathway. A next step is only good if the creator understands the terms and benefits from the outcome.

Pick the smallest honest next step. Do not force every artist into a public launch. Some launches should route to a workshop, partner conversation, publication review, showcase note, or profile page first.
Name the rights boundary. Before public use enters the system, clarify ownership, usage rights, revocation path, attribution, and what AI-assisted assets can or cannot be reused for.
Review the equity outcome. Ask: who benefits, who gets credit, who gains audience, who carries risk, and what would make this launch feel extractive?
