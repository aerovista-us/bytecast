Ep 1 — Divisions and activation lanes

Phase 2 turns the manifesto into a working growth lane. Lumina leads, supporting divisions activate the creator network, and every onboarding step should point to a clear next owner.

Phase 2 moves from awareness to activation. Lumina Creative Media remains the lead division, while EchoVerse Audio, SkyForge Creative Studios, and Vespera Publishing support the growth protocol. Divisions matter because they define who owns the next deliverable, not because they decorate the org chart.

The onboarding flow is explicit. Artists land on the join page, submit their region and medium, move through weekly verification, receive a welcome pack, and then progress from Seed Artist to Regional Beacon to AeroVista Partner as they build trust and visibility.

Activation is sustained with a spotlight framework: a short-form reel, a long-form interview or blog, and an EchoVerse remix. The initial regions are Pacific Northwest, Midwest, Northeast, Southeast, and the Philippines. The point is repeatable proof across regions, not one-off hype.

Growth is measured against real targets: one hundred artist signups in sixty days, ten thousand weekly impressions, five regional showcases, and one partner per region. Division routing only matters if it helps those numbers move.

Slides

Lead and support

“Division” here means execution lane. Lumina leads the campaign while supporting teams deliver the pieces that make activation real.

Lumina. Campaign lead, narrative continuity, and growth orchestration.
EchoVerse. Audio and remix layer for spotlight assets.
SkyForge / Vespera. Showcase formats, stories, and long-form publishing.

The onboarding system is already defined end-to-end, from public intake to verified progression.

Step 1. Intake page captures name, region, medium, tools, and samples.
Step 2. Weekly review issues the Seed Artist badge.
Step 3-4. Welcome pack, community channel, and progression tiers keep momentum visible.

The spotlight series gives each region a repeatable content pattern instead of improvising outreach from scratch.

Short form. Thirty-second reel optimized for mobile discovery.
Long form. Vespera interview or article for context and credibility.
Audio layer. EchoVerse remix extends the creator story into sound.

Phase 2 is only working if signups, impressions, showcases, and regional partners all climb together.

Near-term signal. Early signups, audience feedback, and regional partner interest.
Regional target. Five launch regions with one partner per region.
Operating rule. Route the work toward outcomes, not status labels.

Field guide

Use this when routing artists after they express interest. The best route is the one that matches the artist's goal, the evidence you have, and the division that can responsibly own the next step.

Route by need, not excitement. A musician may need EchoVerse first; a muralist may need Lumina profile copy and Horizon visual capture; a writer may need Vespera publication support.
Capture the minimum useful intake. Region, medium, sample work, consent status, desired outcome, and availability beat a long form nobody finishes.
Define the spotlight artifact. Before promotion, decide whether the first artifact is a reel, profile, remix, interview, pilot page, or event page.
