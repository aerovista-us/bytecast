[INTRO]

The technical overbuilding...

was only half the problem.

[pause]

The deeper problem...

was identity.

[long pause]

And that problem was much harder to diagnose.

---

[SECTION: ON PAPER IT LOOKED IMPRESSIVE]

Because on paper...

having multiple skill sets sounds impressive.

[pause]

Operator.

Builder.

Artist.

Entrepreneur.

Leader.

Technologist.

Strategist.

Creative.

Problem solver.

[pause]

That sounds powerful.

[pause]

And eventually...

it can be.

---

[SECTION: DURING THIS PHASE]

But during this phase?

[pause]

It felt chaotic.

[pause]

Because I kept trying...

to fully become every future version of myself...

at the same time.

[pause]

That was exhausting.

---

[SECTION: PULLED IN EVERY DIRECTION]

One day I felt pulled toward music.

[pause]

The next day toward software.

[pause]

Then infrastructure.

Media.

Real estate.

Business development.

Branding.

Automation.

Leadership.

Education.

AI.

[pause]

And every single one felt real.

[pause]

Every single one felt important.

---

[CORE REALIZATION]

And choosing one path...

started feeling like abandoning another future version of myself.

[pause]

That feeling can be brutal.

---

[SECTION: WHY THIS FELT SO ISOLATING]

Especially when you are highly curious.

[pause]

Especially when you learn quickly.

[pause]

Especially when you can see opportunity...

in multiple directions.

[pause]

Most career advice assumes...

people have one obvious lane.

[pause]

I never felt that.

---

[SECTION: THE INTERNAL QUESTION]

And honestly?

[pause]

That made me feel broken at times.

[pause]

Why couldn’t I just pick one thing?

[pause]

Why did my interests keep expanding...

instead of narrowing?

[pause]

Why did traditional career paths...

feel too small?

---

[SECTION: THE WRONG CONCLUSION]

For a while...

I treated that as a flaw.

[pause]

I thought discipline meant shrinking myself.

[pause]

I thought maturity meant becoming more predictable.

[pause]

I thought success required abandoning huge parts of myself.

---

[CORE REALIZATION]

That created enormous tension.

[pause]

Because every time I tried forcing myself...

into a smaller identity...

it felt wrong.

[long pause]

Not because discipline was wrong.

[pause]

Because the container was wrong.

---

[SECTION: THE BIG REFRAME]

That distinction changed everything.

[pause]

Eventually I realized something massive.

[pause]

The problem wasn’t that I had too many interests.

[pause]

The problem was trying to force all those interests...

into traditional business models.

---

[SECTION: NONE OF THE MODELS FIT]

A traditional startup felt too narrow.

[pause]

A traditional corporate career felt too restrictive.

[pause]

A traditional creative path felt too unstable.

[pause]

A traditional agency felt too limiting.

[pause]

None of them fit the full picture.

---

[SECTION: THE QUESTION THAT CHANGED EVERYTHING]

That realization hurt.

[pause]

But it was also freeing.

[pause]

Because I stopped trying to compress myself...

into existing templates.

[pause]

And started asking a better question.

[long pause]

What kind of company...

would allow all these skill sets...

to strengthen each other?

---

[CORE REALIZATION]

That question eventually became AeroVista.

[pause]

Not because AeroVista solved all my problems.

[pause]

But because it gave my skill stack...

a legitimate operating model.

---

[SECTION: EVERYTHING HAD A PLACE]

Suddenly...

the builder mattered.

[pause]

The artist mattered.

[pause]

The operator mattered.

[pause]

The strategist mattered.

[pause]

The educator mattered.

[pause]

The infrastructure obsession mattered.

[pause]

The storytelling mattered.

[pause]

The automation obsession mattered.

[pause]

The resilience obsession mattered.

---

[CORE REALIZATION]

I didn’t need to become less multidimensional.

[pause]

I needed a business model...

that could handle multidimensional capability.

---

[CLOSING]

That realization changed how I viewed myself forever.

[pause]

But there was still one major problem.

[pause]

Even after I accepted complexity...

I still had unrealistic standards.

[long pause]

[pause]

Perfection.
